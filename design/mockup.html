What this means

We went looking for a security hole — attacks slipping past guardrails when written in Tanglish. We didn't find one. Detection held flat at 98%, and we're reporting that rather than burying it.

The failure isn't that attackers get in. It's that ordinary people get shut out.

Method — 299 Tensor Trust attacks and 150 NotInject benign prompts, each rewritten into natural Roman-script Tamil–English with operative strings and trigger words preserved. Matched by en_source_id; the English baseline is filtered to the same IDs.

Verification — all 23 attack flips were read side-by-side with their English source by a native Tamil speaker, a pass that caught a real character-level bug in 27 rows. The 69 benign flips had automated validation only.

Limits — we measure detector response, not whether an attack would succeed against a live model. This covers Tamil–English specifically, not code-switching in general. Training distribution is the likely cause, but this benchmark measures behaviour, not cause.
